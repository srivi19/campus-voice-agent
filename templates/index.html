What are UTK students complaining about most?
🤖
Top complaints at UTK center on grading inconsistency, unclear expectations, and professors who are hard to reach outside class.

Prof. Alan Becker (Mathematics): "The exams had nothing to do with the homework. Half the class failed the midterm and she refused to curve."

Prof. Daniel Ross (Chemistry): "Lectures are just him reading off PowerPoints. No examples, no engagement. If you ask a question he looks annoyed."

Prof. Kevin Shaw (Engineering): "He lost my final project and gave me a zero without checking. Took weeks to fix."

Pattern across 15+ reviews: Engineering and Chemistry have the most negative mentions. Students consistently flag professors who are unavailable outside class and courses where exams don't match the syllabus.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What data does CampusVoice use? ▼
CampusVoice uses 6,773 real student reviews from Rate My Professors, covering professor quality, grading, course difficulty, and teaching style across 7 US universities.
Which universities are covered? ▼
University of Tennessee Knoxville (UTK), Vanderbilt University, Georgia Institute of Technology, University of Florida, University of Michigan, UCLA, and Duke University.
Can I ask about a specific professor? ▼
Yes! Try asking "Is Professor [Last Name] good at [University]?" and CampusVoice will search for reviews of that specific professor.
How accurate are the answers? ▼
Answers are synthesized directly from real student reviews and always include verbatim quotes. The AI identifies patterns across many reviews — accuracy depends on how many reviews exist for the topic you ask about.
How current is the data? ▼
The reviews were collected in June 2026 from Rate My Professors. Data is not updated in real time but reflects the most recent reviews available at collection time.
Can I compare two universities? ▼
Yes! On the All Universities tab, try asking "How does grading at Vanderbilt compare to Georgia Tech?" and CampusVoice will search both schools and give a side-by-side summary.
Is this free to use? ▼
Yes, CampusVoice is completely free. It was built for the Google Cloud Rapid Agent Hackathon 2026.
How does it work technically? ▼
Your question is sent to Gemini 2.5 Flash, which uses the Elastic MCP Server to search an Elasticsearch index of student reviews. Gemini reads the results, identifies patterns, and writes a synthesized answer with real quotes — all in one agent loop.
⚠️ What are the limitations? ▼
Data freshness: Reviews were collected in June 2026 and are not updated in real time. Answers reflect historical sentiment, not live student opinion.

University coverage: Currently limited to 7 US universities (UTK, Vanderbilt, Georgia Tech, UF, Michigan, UCLA, Duke). Other institutions are not covered.

Source bias: All data comes from Rate My Professors, which skews toward students with strong opinions (positive or negative). Silent majorities may be underrepresented.

Scope: Data covers professor quality, grading, workload, and course difficulty only — not housing, dining, sports, admissions, or campus life.
